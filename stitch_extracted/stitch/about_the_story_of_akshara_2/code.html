Est. 1984

The Genesis of Kannada Form.

Akshara is more than a type studio; it is a custodial journey of the Kannada script, bridging centuries of stone inscriptions with the precision of modern digital interfaces.

Timeline

1984: The First Stroke

Our founder, Dr. Kavitha Murthy, began with a single set of bamboo pens and a vision to catalog the vanishing stone-cut scripts of the Hoysala Empire. The studio started as a small desk in a sun-drenched attic in Bengaluru.

Founding Calligraphy

1998: The Pixel Shift

As the digital revolution swept through India, Akshara became the first to digitize the complex conjunct characters of Kannada with high fidelity. We translated ink-traps into vector paths, ensuring the script's soul remained intact on the screen.

2012: Stone to Screen

Our "Heritage Series" was launched, painstakingly recreating the typography found in 12th-century inscriptions. This project received national acclaim for its role in preserving cultural identity through modern design.
