## **Part 1: The PRD/Brief Template (How to Use It)**

When you have your context, you'll fill in the following sections. A "brief" will have less detail, focusing on the high-level points, while a full "PRD" will go much deeper into each section.

---

### **[Your Project Name] - Project Brief / PRD**

*   **Version:** [e.g., 1.0, 0.9 Draft]
*   **Date:** [Date of creation/last update]
*   **Author(s):** [Your Name/Team]
*   **Stakeholders:** [List key internal stakeholders: Engineering Lead, Design Lead, Marketing Lead, Sales Lead, etc.]
*   **Document Type:** [Project Brief / Full PRD]

---

### **1. Executive Summary**
*   *Brief Overview:* A concise, high-level summary of the project. What is it, why are we doing it, and what's the expected outcome? (1-3 paragraphs)

---

### **2. Problem Statement**
*   *What problem are we solving?* Clearly articulate the pain point or opportunity.
    *   **For Whom:** Who experiences this problem (user persona, customer segment)?
    *   **What is the impact of the problem?** (e.g., lost revenue, decreased efficiency, poor user experience).
    *   **Why is this problem important to solve NOW?**

---

### **3. Goals & Objectives**
*   *What do we aim to achieve with this project?* These should be SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound).
    *   **Strategic Goal:** (e.g., Increase customer retention, enter a new market, improve operational efficiency)
    *   **Product Objectives:** (e.g., Increase feature X adoption by Y% within Z months, Reduce customer support tickets related to A by B%, Improve user satisfaction score for B to C by D%)

---

### **4. Target Users / Personas**
*   *Who are we building this for?* Describe the primary users and any secondary users.
    *   **Key Characteristics:** Demographics, behaviors, needs, pain points relevant to this project.
    *   **User Stories (Optional for Brief, Recommended for PRD):** Short, simple descriptions of a feature from the perspective of an end-user.
        *   "As a [type of user], I want to [goal], so that [reason/benefit]."

---

### **5. Proposed Solution / Features (High-Level)**
*   *What are we building to solve the problem and achieve our goals?*
    *   **Core Concept:** A brief description of the solution.
    *   **Key Features (V1/MVP):** List the essential features and functionalities. (For a brief, list 3-5 key features. For a PRD, you might have a detailed feature matrix or user flow descriptions).
    *   **User Experience (UX) Considerations:** Any specific design principles or critical user flows.

---

### **6. Scope Definition**
*   *What is explicitly IN and OUT of this project for V1?* This is critical for managing expectations.
    *   **In Scope:** (e.g., Feature A, B, C; integration with X; support for Y platform)
    *   **Out of Scope:** (e.g., Feature D, E; integration with Z; support for W platform; advanced reporting beyond V1)

---

### **7. Success Metrics & KPIs**
*   *How will we measure if this project is successful?* Directly link back to your objectives.
    *   **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):** (e.g., Feature adoption rate, customer retention, conversion rate, reduction in support tickets, average time on page, Net Promoter Score (NPS), Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) score)
    *   **Measurement Plan:** How and when will these metrics be tracked?

---

### **8. Technical & Design Considerations (High-Level)**
*   *Any significant technical or design implications?*
    *   **Technical:** (e.g., Requires new API, impacts database schema, needs specific third-party integration, performance implications)
    *   **Design:** (e.g., Requires new UI patterns, significant redesign of existing flows, branding guidelines impact)

---

### **9. Dependencies & Risks**
*   *What could hinder our progress, and what do we need from others?*
    *   **Dependencies:** (e.g., Completion of another project, availability of specific team members, external vendor delivery)
    *   **Risks:** (e.g., Technical complexity underestimated, low user adoption, scope creep, integration challenges, security concerns)
    *   **Mitigation Strategies:** How will we address these risks?

---

### **10. High-Level Timeline / Phased Approach**
*   *Rough estimate of project duration or key milestones.* (For a brief, a general timeframe. For a PRD, more detailed phases).
    *   **Phase 1 (Discovery & Design):** [Date Range]
    *   **Phase 2 (Development):** [Date Range]
    *   **Phase 3 (Testing & QA):** [Date Range]
    *   **Launch:** [Target Date]

---

### **11. Open Questions & Assumptions**
*   *What still needs to be clarified, and what are we assuming to be true?*
    *   **Open Questions:** (e.g., Pricing model, legal review requirements, specific user segment validation)
    *   **Assumptions:** (e.g., Existing API can handle load, users will understand new UX flow, marketing resources will be available)

---

## **Part 2: Example PRD/Brief (Hypothetical Context)**

Let's imagine we're building a new feature for an existing online project management tool (like Asana, Jira, Trello).

---

### **Project Name: Unified Priority Inbox** - Project Brief

*   **Version:** 1.0
*   **Date:** October 26, 2023
*   **Author(s):** Alex Product Manager
*   **Stakeholders:** Engineering Lead (Sarah), Design Lead (Ben), Marketing Lead (Emily), CEO (David)
*   **Document Type:** Project Brief

---

### **1. Executive Summary**
This project introduces a "Unified Priority Inbox" to our project management platform. This new feature will aggregate tasks from all projects a user is involved in, allowing them to quickly view, filter, and prioritize their most critical work from a single, centralized location. The goal is to reduce user effort in managing multiple project workloads, increase task completion efficiency, and ultimately enhance overall user satisfaction and retention.

---

### **2. Problem Statement**
Our users, particularly those involved in multiple projects (e.g., Project Managers, Team Leads, Senior Individual Contributors), frequently express frustration with the time and effort required to review and prioritize tasks across various project boards. This leads to missed deadlines, increased stress, and a perception of tool inefficiency. The current system requires manual navigation between projects, making a holistic view of high-priority work difficult. This directly impacts user productivity and could lead to churn for power users.

---

### **3. Goals & Objectives**
*   **Strategic Goal:** Improve the efficiency and satisfaction of power users managing multiple projects, thereby increasing retention and driving organic growth.
*   **Product Objectives (Q1 2024):**
    *   Increase weekly active users of the new Priority Inbox feature by 20% post-launch.
    *   Reduce user-reported "task overload" in feedback surveys by 15% within 3 months of launch.
    *   Improve overall platform NPS by 5 points among users interacting with 3+ projects.

---

### **4. Target Users / Personas**
*   **Primary:**
    *   **Project Managers:** Need to quickly see critical tasks across all projects they oversee to ensure timely delivery and resource allocation.
    *   **Team Leads:** Require an overview of their team's most urgent work, potentially across different projects, to unblock dependencies.
    *   **Senior Individual Contributors:** Manage tasks spanning multiple initiatives and need a consolidated view to prioritize their daily efforts.
*   **User Stories:**
    *   As a Project Manager, I want to see all high-priority tasks assigned to my team members across all my projects in one list, so I can quickly identify bottlenecks.
    *   As a Senior Developer, I want to filter my prioritized tasks by due date and project, so I can plan my sprint workload efficiently.
    *   As a Team Lead, I want to quickly mark tasks as complete or change their priority directly from my inbox, so I don't have to navigate to individual projects.

---

### **5. Proposed Solution / Features (High-Level)**
The "Unified Priority Inbox" will be a new, dedicated view accessible from the main navigation.
*   **Core Concept:** A personalized, actionable feed of tasks from all projects the user is involved in.
*   **Key Features (V1/MVP):**
    1.  **Task Aggregation:** Automatically pulls all open tasks from all projects the user has access to.
    2.  **Smart Prioritization:** Default sort by a combination of task priority (user-defined) and due date (earliest first).
    3.  **Advanced Filtering:** Ability to filter tasks by Project, Assignee, Due Date, Status, Tags, and Custom Fields.
    4.  **Quick Actions:** Mark complete, change priority, reassign, add quick comment directly from the inbox view.
    5.  **Search:** Integrated search functionality within the inbox.

---

### **6. Scope Definition**
*   **In Scope (V1):**
    *   Unified view of tasks across all *workspaces* the user is part of.
    *   Filtering by standard task attributes (project, assignee, due date, status, tags).
    *   Sorting by priority and due date.
    *   Basic quick actions (complete, reassign, change priority, add comment).
    *   Desktop web client only.
*   **Out of Scope (V1):**
    *   Cross-workspace task aggregation (e.g., if a user has separate accounts for different companies).
    *   AI-driven "smart suggestions" for prioritization.
    *   Custom rule-based automation within the inbox (e.g., "if task is high priority and due in 24h, send me a notification").
    *   Mobile app specific features or UI optimizations beyond responsive design.
    *   Advanced reporting *from* the Priority Inbox view.

---

### **7. Success Metrics & KPIs**
*   **Feature Adoption:** % of unique users accessing the Priority Inbox at least once a week.
*   **Engagement:** Average number of actions (complete, reassign, priority change) performed within the inbox per session.
*   **User Satisfaction:** NPS and CSAT scores from in-app surveys targeting users of the feature.
*   **Efficiency:** Monitoring time saved based on qualitative user feedback and potential A/B testing (e.g., comparing task completion times for prioritized tasks).

---

### **8. Technical & Design Considerations (High-Level)**
*   **Technical:** Requires efficient database queries and indexing to aggregate large volumes of tasks quickly across multiple projects. Potential need for new API endpoints. Performance at scale is critical.
*   **Design:** Requires a clear, intuitive UI for filtering and quick actions to avoid overwhelming users with information. Consistency with existing platform design language is essential.

---

### **9. Dependencies & Risks**
*   **Dependencies:**
    *   Backend API team capacity for new data aggregation endpoints.
    *   UX Research team validation of filter design and quick action placement.
*   **Risks:**
    *   **Performance:** Aggregating thousands of tasks for a single user could lead to slow load times.
        *   *Mitigation:* Optimize database queries, consider pagination and progressive loading, rigorous performance testing.
    *   **User Confusion:** Overly complex filtering options or unclear UI could deter adoption.
        *   *Mitigation:* Iterative user testing, clear onboarding, simplified default views.
    *   **Scope Creep:** Temptation to add advanced prioritization logic or automation to V1.
        *   *Mitigation:* Strict adherence to V1 scope, clearly define future phases.

---

### **10. High-Level Timeline / Phased Approach**
*   **Phase 1 (Discovery & Design):** Nov 1 - Nov 30 (User Research, Wireframes, Mockups, Technical Spikes)
*   **Phase 2 (Development - MVP):** Dec 1 - Jan 31 (Backend and Frontend development of core features)
*   **Phase 3 (Testing & QA):** Feb 1 - Feb 15 (Internal QA, Bug Fixing)
*   **Soft Launch / Beta:** Feb 15 - Feb 28 (Launch to select power users for feedback)
*   **Public Launch (V1):** March 1, 2024

---

### **11. Open Questions & Assumptions**
*   **Open Questions:**
    *   How will "priority" be defined and displayed consistently across different projects which might have varying priority schemes?
    *   What are the specific legal/compliance considerations for data aggregation across regions?
    *   What level of "push notifications" or alerts should be integrated with this inbox for critical items?
*   **Assumptions:**
    *   Our existing task data structure is sufficient to support efficient aggregation without major refactoring.
    *   Users will find value in a consolidated view even without advanced AI features in V1.
    *   The marketing team has capacity to support a launch campaign for this feature.

---
